    <section id="practice">
      <div class="section-head">
        <div>
          <div class="section-kicker">Part 4 · Practice and implementation</div>
          <h2>Plan the real experiment correctly</h2>
          <p class="lead">
            The arithmetic is only one part of DOE. The experimental strategy determines whether the effect estimates are credible.
          </p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="accordion">
        <details open>
          <summary>Randomization, replication, blocking, and center points</summary>
          <div>
            <div class="grid-2">
              <div>
                <h4>Randomization</h4>
                <p>Protects effect estimates against time trends, warm-up, tool wear, operator sequence, and other lurking variables.</p>
              </div>
              <div>
                <h4>Replication</h4>
                <p>Repeats treatment combinations independently and supplies an estimate of experimental error.</p>
              </div>
              <div>
                <h4>Blocking</h4>
                <p>Accounts for known nuisance variation, such as day, heat, coil, machine, raw-material lot, or operator.</p>
              </div>
              <div>
                <h4>Center points</h4>
                <p>Provide a curvature check. A two-level model is linear between low and high settings and cannot independently estimate quadratic curvature.</p>
              </div>
            </div>
          </div>
        </details>

        <details>
          <summary>Excel implementation</summary>
          <div>
            <div class="table-wrap">
              <table>
