Case file: recurring low yield strength

A pipe mill records a rise in low-yield-strength tensile failures. The failures are discovered at final testing, but the origin could lie anywhere from steelmaking through thermal processing and testing. Management wants a corrective action within 30 days.

Your mandate: do not confuse the detection point with the origin point, do not call a brainstormed cause “root cause,” and do not close the project until stability and ownership are established.

Success criteria

  • Clear investigation scope
  • Clean traceable evidence
  • Confirmed or strongly supported cause
  • Prioritized and assigned actions
  • Risk-tested schedule
  • Stable post-fix performance

Important: this is a practical workflow, not a law that every project must use every tool. Use the minimum set that answers the required questions with defensible evidence.

Gate 1: evidence quality

  • Can each test be traced to heat, coil, grade, shift, orientation, and time?
  • Are actual process values recorded rather than only targets?
  • Is the measurement system adequate?
  • Are retests and opposite-end samples distinguishable?

Gate 2: causal credibility

  • Was the problem stratified?
  • Was the fishbone scoped to a Pareto-confirmed effect?
  • Were candidate causes tested?
  • Were confounding and physical mechanism considered?

Gate 3: sustainment

  • Is the monitoring chart appropriate?
  • Are baseline limits valid?
  • Are reaction plans explicit?
  • Are owners and review cadence documented?

Professional standard

A negative result is valuable. If data does not support a fishbone branch, check sample size, range restriction, subgrouping, and MSA. Then document the branch as ruled out and test the next hypothesis.

Capstone deliverable

Use this outline for a real project report:

  1. Problem statement and business/customer impact
  2. Process map and measurement plan
  3. Stratified baseline and Pareto focus
  4. Hypothesis map and verification evidence
  5. Confirmed cause and limitations
  6. Corrective-action options and prioritization logic
  7. Responsibility matrix and PDPC
  8. Activity network and critical path
  9. Control chart, reaction plan, and handoff
