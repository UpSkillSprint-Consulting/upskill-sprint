What Is Lean Six Sigma?

What you will learn: the core idea behind Lean Six Sigma and why it matters at work.

Why this matters at work

Lean Six Sigma combines two ideas: removing waste (Lean) and reducing variation (Six Sigma). Together, they give you a structured way to make a process more reliable and less wasteful — whether that process is a factory line, a claims queue, or a monthly report.

Simple explanation

Lean asks: "Is this step adding value, or is it waste?" Six Sigma asks: "How much does this process vary, and can we make it more consistent?" Most real improvement projects use a bit of both.

Real workplace example

A team notices that order processing time ranges from 2 to 9 days with no clear reason. A Lean Six Sigma approach would map the process, measure where time is lost, and target the biggest source of delay first — rather than guessing.

Try it yourself

Pick one recurring task you do. List every step. Mark each step as "value-adding" or "waste." You’ll likely find at least one step you can cut.
