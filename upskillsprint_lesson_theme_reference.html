Recommended Theme

Modern Engineering + Business Learning Hub

Your lessons should feel like a clean engineering training manual combined with a modern business dashboard. The style should be practical, trustworthy, readable, and professional.

Professional

Use navy headings, white cards, clean spacing, and simple explanations.

Practical

Every lesson should include examples, formulas, practice, and workplace application.

Consistent

Keep the same layout across topics. Change only the small topic accent color.

Best positioning: Practical skills for data, quality, Lean Six Sigma, statistics, project management, business decision-making, and AI for work.

Reusable Components

Lesson Building Blocks

These components should appear repeatedly across your lessons. This makes the site feel organized and easy to follow.

Lesson Objectives

  • Explain the concept in simple workplace language.
  • Show the key formula or decision rule.
  • Apply the concept to a realistic example.
  • Check understanding with practice questions.

Recommended Lesson Flow

  1. Start with the problem.
    Explain why the lesson matters at work.
  2. Teach the concept.
    Use simple terms before formulas.
  3. Show the method.
    Use steps, visuals, and examples.
  4. Practice.
    Add quiz questions, calculators, or mini cases.
Formula / Key Rule Box:
Use this style for formulas, DAX measures, SQL logic, hypothesis test rules, capability formulas, or decision rules.
Practice Box: Use this for learner exercises, short quizzes, or “try it yourself” sections.
Common Mistake Box: Use this to explain what learners often misunderstand.
Avoid This: Use this for wrong interpretations, bad data habits, or risky assumptions.

Example Lesson Card

In a real workplace, do not only calculate a metric. Explain what the metric means, what action it supports, and what risk exists if the data is wrong.

Workplace translation: The best lessons should help learners move from calculation to decision-making.

Quality Control

Before Publishing Any Lesson

Use this checklist to keep every lesson useful, clear, and professional.

Content Checklist

  • The lesson starts with a real workplace problem.
  • The learner knows what they will gain.
  • Definitions are simple and practical.
  • Formulas or rules are clearly explained.
  • There is at least one worked example.
  • There is at least one practice activity.

Design Checklist

  • Navy is used for headings and major sections.
  • Teal is used for buttons and key highlights.
  • Cards have enough spacing and clean borders.
  • Topic colors are used only as small accents.
  • The page works on mobile and desktop.
  • The lesson does not look overcrowded.
Best rule: If a learner cannot explain what to do with the lesson at work, the lesson is not finished yet.
